# Phase 13: Resume, LinkedIn & Personal Branding



## Part 1: The Resume – Your "One-Page" Movie Trailer

**Concept:** Your resume is not a list of jobs. It is a marketing document that proves you can solve business problems. A recruiter spends an average of **6 seconds** scanning a resume. You must make those 6 seconds count.

### Why is it important?

* It is the gatekeeper. If your resume is rejected by the ATS or ignored by a recruiter, your skills are irrelevant.

* It sets the narrative. It tells the interviewer *exactly* what to ask you about.

### Advantages & Disadvantages:

| Advantages | Disadvantages |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Controls the Narrative:** You frame your experience in the best light. | **Time-Consuming:** Perfecting a resume requires 20+ iterations. |
| **Serves as a Cheat Sheet:** You can refer to it during interviews. | **Generic is Fatal:** A one-size-fits-all resume gets you nowhere. |

***

### Best Practices (The "Sterling" Resume Rules):

**1. The ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Rules:**

* **Format:** Save as a .docx or .pdf. (Check the job description—some ATS systems prefer .docx).

* **Keywords:** The ATS scans for specific words. Copy the keywords directly from the job description (e.g., "SQL," "Power BI," "Data Visualization," "Stakeholder Management").

* **Avoid:** Tables, images, logos, and columns. The ATS cannot read them. Use a simple, single-column layout.

* **Headers:** Use standard headers: "Summary," "Skills," "Experience," "Education," "Projects."



**2. The "Sterling" Resume Structure (1 Page Maximum - 2 Pages MAX if senior):**

**Section 1: Header (Top 20% of the page)**

```text
    [Your Full Name]
    [Phone Number] | [Email Address] | [LinkedIn URL] | [GitHub URL]
    [City, State] (Optional - only include if applying locally)
```

**Section 2: Professional Summary (The "Hook")**

* ***What it is:*** 2–3 sentences that summarize your value proposition.

* ***The Formula:*** `[Job Title]` with `[X]` years of experience in `[Industry/Function]`. Proven track record of `[Key Achievement]` using `[Top 3 Skills]` to drive `[Business Outcome]`.

* ***Bad Example:*** "Hardworking analyst looking for a challenging role."

* ***Good Example:*** "Data Analyst with 4+ years of experience in E-commerce and Retail Analytics. Proven track record of reducing customer churn by 8% ($1.2M in retained revenue) using SQL, Python, and Power BI. Expert at translating complex data into actionable executive insights."



**Section 3: Core Competencies / Skills (The Keywords Section)**

* **Why:** This is where the ATS grabs its keywords.

* **Structure:** A simple bulleted or comma-separated list.

* **Example:**

    * **Data Visualization:** Power BI, Tableau, DAX.
    
    * **Programming & Databases:** SQL (PostgreSQL, MySQL), Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn), Excel (Advanced).
    
    * **Analytics:** ETL, Data Cleaning, A/B Testing, Predictive Modeling, Cohort Analysis.
    
    * **Soft Skills:** Stakeholder Management, Storytelling, Executive Presentations.
    

**Section 4: Professional Experience (The Meat)**

* ***The Formula (STAR):*** Situation, Task, Action, Result.

* ***The "XY" Rule for Bullet Points:*** "Accomplished **[X]** as measured by **[Y]** by doing **[Z]**."

* ***Structure:***


```text
**Job Title** | Company Name | Date Range

* Bullet Point 1: [Action] + [Result (quantified)] + [Tools Used].
* Bullet Point 2: [Action] + [Result (quantified)] + [Tools Used].
* Bullet Point 3: [Action] + [Result (quantified)] + [Tools Used].
```

* **Weak Bullet:** "Built dashboards."

* **Strong Bullet:** "Built an interactive Power BI sales dashboard that tracked regional performance, enabling the VP of Sales to identify underperforming territories and increase Q3 revenue by 12% ($3.2M)."

**Section 5: Projects (The Portfolio Bridge)**

* **Why:** If you lack formal work experience, this section is your salvation.

* **Structure:** Same as Experience. Link directly to your GitHub or dashboard.

* **Example:**

    * **Predictive Churn Analysis (Python, Power BI):** Developed a Logistic Regression model achieving 82% accuracy in identifying at-risk telecom customers. Delivered a Power BI dashboard that reduced churn by 8% ($1.2M saved).

    * **Retail Sales Performance Command Center (SQL, Power BI):** Architected a Star Schema data model to analyze $50M in transactions. Designed an executive dashboard tracking inventory turnover and gross margin.


### Common Mistakes Beginners Make:

* **Using "I" or "My":** Resumes should be implicit. "Led" not "I led."

* **Job Descriptions, Not Accomplishments:** Don't tell me what you were supposed to do. Tell me what you *actually* achieved.

* **Typos:** A single typo signals carelessness. Proofread it 5 times. Then get someone else to proofread it.

## Part 2: LinkedIn Optimization – Your Digital Handshake

**Concept:** LinkedIn is the global directory of professionals. Recruiters use it to *hunt* for talent. Your profile must be a living, breathing version of your resume.


### Why is it important?

* **Visibility:** 90% of recruiters use LinkedIn to source candidates.

* **Passive Income:** An optimized profile gets recruiters approaching *you*, saving you from the grind of applying to 100 jobs.


### The "Sterling" LinkedIn Checklist:

**1. Profile Photo:** Professional headshot. No selfies, no group photos. (Spend $50 to get a professional one—worth every penny).

**2. Headline (The Biggest Bang for Buck):**

* ***Bad:*** "Data Analyst"

* ***Good:*** "Data Analyst | SQL, Power BI, Python | I turn messy data into clear business decisions"

* ***Best:*** "Data Analyst @ [Company] | Ex-[Previous] | Driving $10M+ in revenue through Data-Driven Strategies"

**3. About Section (Your "Story"):** Write 3-4 short paragraphs.

* Paragraph 1: Who you are and what you do.

* Paragraph 2: Your technical skills and a specific achievement.

* Paragraph 3: Your philosophy (e.g., "I believe data should tell a story.").

* Paragraph 4: A call to action ("Open to opportunities in the Healthcare space. DM me!").

**4. Featured Section:** This is the most underutilized feature. Pin your top 3 projects directly to the top of your profile. Attach a PDF of your dashboard or a link to your GitHub.

**5. Experience Section:** Same as your resume, but slightly more conversational.

**6. Recommendations:** Reach out to former managers or colleagues and ask them to write a short recommendation. It builds instant trust.

**7. Activity:** Engage with posts in your industry. Share an interesting article and write a 2-sentence opinion. This keeps you on the radar.



### Common Mistakes:

* Having a blank or generic "About" section.

* Not having a profile photo.

* Treating LinkedIn like a static resume (you should post/engage at least once a month).

## Part 3: The Case Study – Your "Deep Dive" Document

**Concept:** A Case Study is a 5-10 page document (or a long LinkedIn article) that walks through one of your portfolio projects from *problem to solution*.

### Why is it important?

When a hiring manager asks, "Tell me about a time you solved a complex problem," you hand them the Case Study. It proves you can think critically and communicate clearly.

### The "Sterling" Case Study Structure:

**Title:** `[Project Name]` - Using `[Technology]` to Solve `[Business Problem]`

**1. Executive Summary (1 paragraph):**

* "ConnectTel was losing 15% of its customers annually. I built a predictive churn model using Python and Power BI that identified at-risk customers, resulting in an 8% reduction in churn ($1.2M saved)."

**2. Business Problem (1-2 paragraphs):**

* "The retention team was reactive, only calling customers after they canceled. We needed a proactive model to identify churn signals early."

**3. Data & Methodology (2-3 paragraphs):**

* "The dataset consisted of 7,000 customer records, with 20+ features. I used Pandas for cleaning, handled missing values via median imputation, and applied Logistic Regression due to its interpretability. The model achieved 82% AUC-ROC."


**4. Dashboard / Visualization (1 page of visuals):**

* Screenshots of your Power BI dashboard with annotations explaining the key insights.

**5. Impact & Recommendations (1 paragraph):**

* "The model identified 'Month-to-Month' contracts and 'High Monthly Charges' as primary drivers. I recommended converting these users to annual plans with a 10% discount, which led to an 8% reduction in churn."

**6. Reflection (1 paragraph):**

* "If I were to rebuild this, I would incorporate a wider range of engagement metrics and use a Random Forest to test if I could push accuracy above 85%."

**Advantages:** A case study transforms you from a "coder" to a "consultant." It shows you can write, think, and deliver.

### Common Mistakes:

* Making it too technical. (Don't show me your code; show me your *thinking*).

* Not including visuals.

* Forgetting the business impact.

## Part 4: The Portfolio Website (The "Professional" Edge)

**Concept:** A personal website that aggregates your resume, case studies, and portfolio.

### Why is it important?

* It signals you are serious. It is the difference between "someone looking for a job" and "a professional."

* It centralizes everything: LinkedIn, GitHub, Case Studies, and Contact Information.


### How to Build It (The 80/20 Way):

**1. Platform:** GitHub Pages (free), Netlify (free), or Carrd.co (simple templates).

**2. Structure:**

* **Home:** A welcome message + professional photo.

* **About:** Your story (1 paragraph).

* **Projects:** Links to your 3 Golden Projects + Case Studies.

* **Contact:** A simple form or your email.

**3. Examples:** Look up "Data Analyst Portfolio" on Google. Emulate the ones you like.


### Common Mistakes:

* Over-engineering it. A clean, simple site is better than a flashy, broken one.

* Not updating it.

## Part 5: The "Sterling" Job Application Strategy

**Concept:** Spraying your resume to 500 jobs is a waste of time. A targeted strategy gets you hired faster.

### The "50-10-5" Rule:

* **50:** Apply to 50 "Target B" jobs (companies you like, but aren't obsessed with) using a *generic* version of your resume. This builds your application muscle.

* **10:** Apply to 10 "Target A" jobs (your dream companies). For these, *customize* your resume to match the job description exactly (mirror their keywords).

* **5:** Reach out to 5 hiring managers directly on LinkedIn for your Target A jobs. Send a personalized connection request.


**The "Follow-Up" Email Template (1 Week After Applying):**

```text
    Subject: Following Up - [Job Title] Application - [Your Name]

    Hi [Hiring Manager Name],

    I hope you are having a productive week.
```

## Interview Questions (Resume & LinkedIn Specific)

**1. Beginner: "Tell me about your resume gap / Why did you leave your last job?"**

* ***Expected Answer:*** Be honest but positive. "I was looking for an opportunity to deepen my analytical skills and move into a more data-intensive role. I used that time to complete a rigorous data analytics certification and build my portfolio, which you can see on my GitHub."

**2. Intermediate: "Your resume says you 'reduced churn by 8%.' How did you measure that?"**

* ***Expected Answer:*** I used a cohort analysis. I compared the churn rate of customers before the model was implemented (a 3-month baseline) versus the churn rate after we started targeting the at-risk customers with retention offers. The 8% reduction was the difference between the two periods, normalized for seasonality.

**3. Advanced: "What is one thing on your resume you wish you could do over?"**

* ***Expected Answer:*** Show humility. "I wish I had documented my early projects more thoroughly. Some of my older notebooks are messy. Now, I always write a clean README and structure my code with clear comments. I learned that clarity is kindness to the next person who reads it."

**4. Scenario: "Your LinkedIn profile says you are 'Open to Work.' I have a role that is not an exact match for your experience. Why are you applying?"**

* ***Expected Answer:*** "I am open to opportunities where I can apply my core analytics skills—SQL, Python, and Dashboarding—even if the industry is different. My portfolio shows I can pick up domain knowledge quickly. I am particularly drawn to this role because of [Company's Product/Mission]."
